About me

I love messy problems - where the situation is unclear, people are pulling in different directions, and the team needs clarity to move forward.

My default approach is:

  • understand the user and the story
  • validate with data
  • structure the problem space
  • align stakeholders once there's enough signal
  • execute with focus and momentum

I currently work at a HRTech payroll product in Bengaluru, KA, India.

I have previously worked in Mobility and Analytics domains.

I am domain agnostic and a generalist PM who brings structure, soul, and a quietly stubborn instinct for doing what's right. Even when it's not easy.

What I value

Teamwork over hero culture

I don't believe in lone wolves. Good products are built in the messy middle – with engineers, design, ops, and whoever else the business needs. Everyone gets their voice. I care about shared context, shared ownership, and making sure the team wins together. If something fails, accountability should be shared. If something fails, accountability should be collective and constructive - never personal.

Communication out loud

I've learned that most execution failures aren't because people are incapable — they're because people aren't aligned. I over-index on saying things early, clearly, explicitly: what we're solving, what we're not solving, why a decision was made, and what tradeoffs we're accepting. I'd rather risk sounding repetitive than let teams silently diverge. Clarity upfront saves weeks of confusion later.

Psychological safety

I take team trust seriously. People do their best work when they're not afraid of being blamed, ridiculed, or undermined. I try to create an environment where it's safe to ask "stupid questions," challenge ideas, admit mistakes early, and disagree respectfully. I don't throw people under the bus — especially in front of leadership — and I actively give credit where it's due, because trust compounds over time.

Due diligence

I don't like wasting other people's time. Before escalating a question or seeking help, I try to do the work: understand the context, check data, attempt solutions, and narrow down what I'm actually stuck on. It makes conversations sharper, decisions faster, and it builds credibility with teams. Also — you learn far more by trying first than by outsourcing your thinking.
